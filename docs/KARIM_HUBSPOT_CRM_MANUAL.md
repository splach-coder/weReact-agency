# WeReact HubSpot CRM Manual

## Owner

**Client and sales owner:** Karim Hida  
**Website delivery owner:** Anas / WeReact development

Karim owns the relationship from the first enquiry through the signed agreement. Anas owns production after the sale. Karim remains the client's main communication contact during delivery.

## The two boards

### Sales Pipeline - Karim

1. **New enquiry** - A new website enquiry has arrived and has not been contacted.
2. **Contact attempted** - Karim has called, emailed, or sent a WhatsApp message.
3. **Connected** - The prospect has replied and a conversation is active.
4. **Qualified** - The business has a real need, budget, decision-maker, and useful timeline.
5. **Discovery booked** - A discovery call has a confirmed date and time.
6. **Proposal sent** - The prospect received a written scope, price, and next step.
7. **Negotiation** - Scope, price, timing, or payment terms are being discussed.
8. **Closed won** - The client accepted the offer and the project is ready for delivery.
9. **Closed lost** - The opportunity is not moving forward. Always record the reason.

### Website Delivery - Anas

1. **Client handoff**
2. **Kickoff & deposit**
3. **Content received**
4. **Design**
5. **Development**
6. **Client review**
7. **Final payment**
8. **Website launched**
9. **Aftercare**
10. **Completed**

## Daily routine

Start every working day in HubSpot.

1. Open Deals and filter for open opportunities.
2. Contact every **New enquiry** as quickly as possible. Target: within 15 minutes during working hours and never later than the same business day.
3. Complete overdue tasks before starting cold outreach.
4. Move every deal to its true stage after each call or message.
5. Add a short note describing what happened and the agreed next action.
6. Create the next follow-up task before leaving the record.
7. End the day with no deal missing a next action or follow-up date.

## New enquiry checklist

The website creates the contact and deal automatically. Before contacting the lead, confirm:

- Full name
- Company
- Email
- Direct phone number
- WhatsApp number, when supplied
- Project message
- Lead source and campaign attribution
- Deal owner

Do not create a duplicate contact. Search by email and phone before adding anyone manually.

## First contact

Use the channel the lead made easiest. WhatsApp or phone is preferred when a number is available.

Suggested opening:

> Hi [Name], this is Karim from WeReact. We received your enquiry about [project/business]. I would like to understand what you need and see whether we are the right fit. Is now a good time for a quick conversation?

If there is no answer:

- First attempt: immediately
- Second attempt: later the same day
- Third attempt: next business day
- Final follow-up: three business days later

After the final unanswered follow-up, leave a note and close the deal as lost with the reason **No response**.

## Qualification questions

Karim should understand these points before using **Qualified**:

1. What does the business sell, and who is the ideal customer?
2. What problem should the website solve?
3. Is there an existing website?
4. Which pages, languages, integrations, or booking features are required?
5. What is the desired launch date?
6. What budget range is available?
7. Who approves the project?
8. What would make the project successful after launch?

Do not promise price, scope, or delivery dates that Anas has not approved.

## Notes and tasks

Every meaningful interaction needs a note. Use this format:

```text
Summary:
Need:
Budget/timeline:
Decision-maker:
Objections:
Agreed next step:
Next follow-up date:
```

Every open deal must have one clear next task. Examples: call lead, send case study, prepare estimate, follow up on proposal, confirm deposit.

## Proposal rules

Move a deal to **Proposal sent** only when the client has received:

- Agreed project scope
- Deliverables
- Price and currency
- Payment schedule
- Estimated timeline
- Client responsibilities
- Revision limits
- Clear acceptance step

Set the deal amount and expected close date before sending the proposal.

## Closed-won handoff

When the client accepts:

1. Move the deal to **Closed won**.
2. Confirm the agreed price, currency, scope, deposit, and target launch date on the deal.
3. Create a ticket in **Website Delivery** at **Client handoff**.
4. Associate the ticket with the client contact and the won deal.
5. Assign the delivery ticket to Anas.
6. Add a handoff note containing the final scope, promises, deadlines, client preferences, and risks.
7. Introduce Anas to the client and confirm who handles technical questions.
8. Keep ownership of client communication unless Anas requests otherwise.

Handoff is complete only when Anas confirms that the information is sufficient.

## During delivery

Karim should:

- Keep the client informed without interrupting development unnecessarily.
- Collect missing content and approvals.
- Record feedback in the delivery ticket instead of scattered messages.
- Confirm that requested changes are inside the signed scope.
- Ask Anas before promising revisions, features, or delivery dates.
- Help secure final payment before launch when required by the agreement.

Anas moves the Website Delivery ticket through the production stages.

## Closed-lost rules

Never delete a lost opportunity. Move it to **Closed lost** and record one reason:

- No response
- Budget too low
- Timing not ready
- Chose another provider
- Project cancelled
- Not a good fit
- Duplicate or spam

Add a future task when the prospect may become relevant later.

## Non-negotiable CRM rules

- HubSpot is the source of truth.
- No open deal without an owner, stage, note, and next task.
- No important promise only in WhatsApp or email; summarize it in HubSpot.
- Do not create duplicate contacts or deals.
- Do not move a deal forward to make the pipeline look better.
- Protect client information and never share access credentials in notes.
- Review stale deals weekly and close or advance them honestly.

## Weekly review with Anas

Once a week, review:

- New enquiries and response times
- Qualified opportunities
- Proposals awaiting decisions
- Deals with no activity for seven days
- Expected revenue and close dates
- Closed-lost reasons
- Won projects waiting for handoff
- Delivery tickets blocked by client content or approval

The goal is simple: every client knows the next step, and every WeReact project has one accountable owner.
